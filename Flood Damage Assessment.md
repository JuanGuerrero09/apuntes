## Understanding disaster risk

$$
Risk = f(hazard, \,consquence)
$$
**Risk and its constituent components**

- Disaster Risk (Floods, drought, seismic, etc)
	- Hazard (Probability and severity)
	- Exposure (Structures, Population, Economy)
	- Vulnerability (Physical, Social, Environmental, Economic)
	- Capacity and measures (Physical planning, Social capacity)
- Source - Pathway - Receptor - Consequence

## Typology of damage

Tangible: Quantifiable Damage (in monetary terms)
Intangible: Damage that cannot be quantified (in monetary terms)

x

Direct: occurs due to direct contact with water
Indirect: Occurs as a consequence of flood event


Tangible Direct: Buildings damages, infrastructure, crop damage
Tangible Indirect: Loss of production time due a loss of a bridge, financial impacts (stock market)

Intangible Direct: Loss of life, Evacuation, Cultural losses
Intangible Indirect: Unhappy employees, Migration, Impact of cultural losses


Relationship between probability of water leven and damage
- Assumption $P(flood) = P(Damage)$

Small floods:
- Annual probability of occurrence: high (e.g. $p = 0.8$)
- Impacts of small floods: los, around 150K

Medium floods:
- Annual probability: medium (e.g. $p=0.5$)
- Impacts: medium, around 2.5M

Big floods:
- Annual probability of occurrence: low (e.g. $p = 0.1$)
- Consequences: high, around 10M

To calculate estimating expected annual losses due to flooding:

Flood level (h) - P(h) x D(h) = P(h) * D(h)
low - 0.8 - 150K = 120K
medium - 0.5 - 2.5M = 1.25M
high - 0.1 - 10M = 1M
