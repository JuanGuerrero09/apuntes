## **Team Building History**

### **(1) Reflections from NAIC**
The starting point for this team was the "Ice Rider Calyrex + Kyogre" build I brought to NAIC in June. While I won't go into full detail, that team used a high-power Trick Room (TR) core supported by Smeargle, Incineroar, and Amoonguss, allowing for aggressive plays regardless of whether TR was active.

I finished 9-3, and other players using the team had similar results. The flaws were clear:

**Strengths discovered:**
1.  **Fairy Tera + Leech Seed Ice Rider:** Resisting Fighting and being neutral to Ghost/Psychic made it strong against Zamazenta without being weak to Shadow Rider. It has great synergy with Smeargle; Leech Seed forces switches, which lets Smeargle land Spores or farm *Moody* boosts.
2.  **Smeargle’s Viability:** Only Miraidon (via Dazzling Gleam) consistently outspeeds and hits Smeargle with spread moves. This makes Focus Sash Smeargle very hard to KO before it acts.
3.  **Double Fake Out + Ice Rider:** Having two Fake Out users allows for flexible setup of TR or Leech Seed recovery turns. It also stabilizes the matchup against Choice item Miraidon.

**Weaknesses discovered:**
4.  **Underestimating Urshifu:** The Ice Rider + Kyogre duo was slow at dealing with Water Urshifu. Even with Amoonguss and Water-resist Teras, Choice Band variants often overwhelmed the team.
5.  **Lack of Answers to Smeargle Counters:** The team struggled against Grass-types and Grass Tera users designed to wall Smeargle. I expected Ice Rider to handle them, but the surrounding team members often made the board state too difficult.

### **(2) Smeargle in the Sun**
To keep the strengths of Smeargle while fixing the Urshifu and Grass-type issues, I replaced Kyogre with **Koraidon**. 
*   In the Sun, Smeargle’s Focus Sash cannot be broken by a single *Surging Strikes* from Water Urshifu. 
*   Koraidon’s Fire-type attacks provide an immediate answer to Grass-types.
*   Koraidon’s high power and *Flame Charge* speed boosts are effective against Shadow Rider builds. 

I kept Ice Rider Calyrex because it covers Koraidon’s weaknesses to Tailwind (Tornadus/Whimsicott) and the Koraidon mirror match.

### **(3) Adopting a Trick Room Sweeper**
Switching to Koraidon solved the previous issues but created a new one: Koraidon is not effective under Trick Room. This lowered the team's "TR pressure," making it harder to clean up Zamazenta or Incineroar. 

I looked back at a Reg G Ice Rider team I used for the Brisbane Regional, which utilized **Landorus-Incarnate** alongside Ice Rider. Landorus-I has a perfect movepool to complement Ice Rider’s ice moves, specifically hitting Zamazenta and Incineroar. Additionally, the "Follow Me + Landorus" lead is a proven strategy against Miraidon.

### **(4) The Role of Fake Out**
To fulfill the "Double Fake Out" strength, I initially tested **Rillaboom** and **Incineroar** in the final two slots. 
*   **Rillaboom:** Essential for Miraidon matchups (Terrain control) and providing priority.
*   **Incineroar:** Essential for Intimidate support and cycling *Parting Shot*. 

Crucially, having Incineroar on the team allowed me to take *Fake Out* off Smeargle. This freed up a move slot for **Needle Guard** (Spiky Shield). In an Open Team Sheet (OTS) environment, a Smeargle that can *Protect* while farming *Moody* boosts or scouting moves is incredibly difficult for the opponent to handle.

*   **Logic:** Speed is tuned to outrun max speed Zamazenta. Bulk is set to survive a Choice Specs Miraidon *Draco Meteor* (outside of Terrain) when Tera-Fire. Clear Amulet is mandatory to ignore Intimidate and *Snarl*.

### **Calyrex (Ice Rider)**
*   **Item:** Leftovers | **Ability:** As One | **Tera:** Fairy
*   **Nature:** Brave | **Stats:** 207(252)-210(44)-170(0)-*-177(212)-57 (Speed IV: 19)
*   **Moves:** Glacial Lance, Leech Seed, Trick Room, Protect
*   **Logic:** A bulk-heavy D-investment build to survive Miraidon and Shadow Rider. Speed is 57 to be exactly 1 point slower than minimum speed Incineroar and Farigiraf.

*   **Item:** Life Orb | **Ability:** Sheer Force | **Tera:** Water
*   **Nature:** Modest | **Stats:** 187(180)-*-113(20)-182(244)-101(4)-129(60)
*   **Moves:** Earth Power, Sludge Bomb, Sandsear Storm, Protect
*   **Logic:** Hits like a restricted Pokemon. Tera Water is defensive, primarily to survive *Glacial Lance* from opposing Ice Riders or *Aqua Step*.

*   **Logic:** Safety Goggles for Amoonguss/Smeargle. EV'd to survive +1 Life Orb *Astral Barrage*. S-IV 25 ensures it moves after most opposing Incineroar for *Parting Shot* positioning.

### **Rillaboom**

## **Selection and Matchup Guide**

### **VS Shadow Rider Calyrex + Zamazenta**
There are three common variations of this team:

**1. Against Smeargle / Raikou / Rillaboom**
*   **Lead:** Ice Rider + Smeargle | **Back:** Landorus + Rillaboom
*   **Turn 1:** Target Smeargle with *Spore* and use Fairy Tera *Glacial Lance*. 
    *   If you win the Smeargle speed tie: Huge advantage. Set TR next turn.
    *   If Ice Rider is slept: If their Smeargle is also slept, try to sleep their Shadow Rider next. You can usually recover via TR unless they get a 1-turn wake.
    *   If Smeargle is slept: Ice Rider's *Glacial Lance* chips them. Next turn: "Protect + Swap Smeargle for Rillaboom" -> "TR + U-turn."

**2. Against Incineroar / Rillaboom / Hearthflame Ogerpon**
*   **Lead:** Koraidon + Smeargle | **Back:** Landorus + Incineroar
*   **Logic:** This variant usually leads Shadow Rider/Incineroar. 
*   **Turn 1:** Use *Needle Guard* pressure. Common play: "Tera Fire Flame Charge (into Incineroar)" + "Spore (into Shadow Rider)." 

**3. Against Chien-Pao / Dragonite (Taunt expected)**
*   **Lead 1:** Koraidon + Ice Rider | **Back:** Smeargle + Landorus
*   **Lead 2:** Ice Rider + Incineroar | **Back:** Smeargle + Landorus
*   **Strategy:** Treat Chien-Pao as the priority. "Flame Charge + Fairy Tera Glacial Lance" to remove the cat, then set TR. 

### **VS Miraidon + Lunala**
*   **Selection 1:** Koraidon + Rillaboom | **Back:** Incineroar + Landorus
*   **Selection 2:** Ice Rider + Rillaboom | **Back:** Incineroar + Landorus
*   **Logic:** Against Specs Miraidon, double Fake Out is brutal. 
    *   Turn 1 options: (1) Protect + U-turn into the Miraidon slot, or (2) Fake Out + Tera Fire *Low Kick* into Miraidon. 
    *   Against Life Orb Miraidon: Play more conservatively with *Protect* to scout for their *Protect*.
*   Selection 2 is for later games in a Bo3. Use Fairy Tera + Leech Seed to make Ice Rider unkillable while chipping away at Lunala's *Shadow Guard*.

### **VS Miraidon + Ice Rider**
*   **Selection 1:** Rillaboom + Landorus | **Back:** Ice Rider + Incineroar
*   **Selection 2:** Koraidon + Landorus | **Back:** Ice Rider + Rillaboom
*   **Logic:** Against Farigiraf leads, use "Protect + U-turn" into Incineroar/Ice Rider and use Tera Water Landorus to fire off *Earth Powers*. 
*   Selection 2 handles Fire Tera Ice Rider by using Koraidon's Fire moves and Landorus's Ground moves to "pincer" the opponent regardless of their Tera.
