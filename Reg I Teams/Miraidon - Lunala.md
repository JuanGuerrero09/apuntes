### **■ Team Building History**

#### **What team has the highest probability of winning the championship?**

From the end of the Japan National Championships until two weeks before Worlds, I spent all my time thinking about what team could actually win the title. I required two conditions for the core of the team:

1. The power to win even against unfavorable matchups in a tournament setting, provided one plays perfectly.
    
2. The meta environment on the day of the event should not be a "headwind" (unfavorable) for the team.
    

By the end of the _Terurun Festival_, the candidates that satisfied the first condition were narrowed down to two:

- **Shadow Rider Calyrex + Zamazenta** (Standard build centered on Life Orb Shadow Rider).
    
- **Lunala + Miraidon** (The six-Pokémon core that won NAIC).
    

For the **Shadow Rider + Zamazenta** core, I felt its strength wasn't just in the raw power and defensive synergy of the two restricted Pokémon, but in the strategy of treating them carefully, exhausting the opponent's resources, and then sweeping with one of the legends. It also had high customization potential.

For the **Lunala + Miraidon** core, I valued its incredibly wide range of selection and board-state options, allowing for a "skill-based" match against almost any build. If you can switch Urshifu and Ursaluna onto the field at the perfect moment, you can win against almost anything. From the opponent's perspective, there are so many things to play around that it puts immense pressure on them over a BO3 set.

Next, I considered the second condition (the meta environment).

**Shadow Rider + Zamazenta had two major issues:**

- It is unstable in mirror matches without Smeargle.
    
- It tends to struggle against builds involving Chi-Yu.
    

Regarding the mirror: At the Terurun Festival, a Smeargle variant strong in mirrors took top ranks. Furthermore, Modest Shadow Rider (which can OHKO Zamazenta with +2 Life Orb Psychic) increased, making it hard to gain an edge through build differences alone. The mirror meta was becoming too volatile.

Regarding Chi-Yu: A new build of **Ice Rider Calyrex + Koraidon** took runner-up at the Terurun Festival and spread quickly. I also felt that **Groudon + Ice Rider** teams, which easily incorporate Chi-Yu, were on the rise. Solving these two issues while maintaining a win rate against existing teams seemed nearly impossible, so I abandoned Shadow Rider + Zamazenta.

#### **The Life Orb Miraidon Revolution**

On the other hand, Lunala + Miraidon’s difficult matchups were:

- Ghost Tera Focus Sash Shadow Rider + Zamazenta (with Chien-Pao/Amoonguss).
    
- Fairy Tera Shadow Rider + Clefairy/Incineroar/Rillaboom.
    

Against **Ice Rider + Miraidon + Focus Sash Urshifu**, the game often came down to a Turn 1 Tera-read, which I wasn't confident in. While the Sash Shadow Rider variant won the Terurun Festival and increased in number, it was within a tolerable range. Just as I was leaning toward Lunala + Miraidon, a "revolution" occurred: using **Life Orb Miraidon** with _Protect_ instead of Choice Specs.

Life Orb Miraidon was often seen in Ice Rider + Miraidon builds, but trying it in this core felt amazing. I reached Top 8 in a Victory Road tournament with it and felt it was so strong I couldn't understand why it wasn't popular.

- _Protect_ punishes double-targeting from Shadow Rider, Zamazenta, or Koraidon + Fake Out.
    
- Electric Tera avoids the "speed tie" guess against Scarf Miraidon.
    
- Stellar-boosted (or Electric Tera) _Electro Drift_ OHKOs Zamazenta.
    
- It can fire off a _Draco Meteor_ and then _Volt Switch_ out.
    

This revolution improved the win rates against difficult matchups and gave me the confidence to lock in Lunala + Miraidon. I spent the last two weeks refining EVs and playstyles without needing to change the roster.

_(Note: I had ruled out Koraidon as a winning choice two weeks prior, which turned out to be wrong, but I believe my team choice still gave me a real shot at winning.)_

### **■ Individual Analysis**

#### **Miraidon**

- **Nature:** Modest
    
- **Stats:** 179-*-124-198-136-187
    
- **EVs:** 28 HP / 28 Def / 196 SpA / 4 SpD / 252 Spe
    
- **Tera Type:** Electric
    
- **Item:** Life Orb
    
- **Moves:** Electro Drift, Draco Meteor, Volt Switch, Protect
    
- **Logic:** Modest was chosen for a chance to OHKO Ice Rider Calyrex or AV Incineroar. Max speed (187) was used because many Miraidons aimed at outspeeding Zamazenta (181–184) were in that range.
    

#### **Lunala**

- **Nature:** Modest
    
- **Stats:** 240-*-118-205-137-116
    
- **EVs:** 220 HP / 36 Def / 236 SpA / 12 SpD / 0 Spe (Speed IV: 29)
    
- **Tera Type:** Fairy
    
- **Item:** Electric Seed
    
- **Moves:** Moongeist Beam, Moonblast, Wide Guard, Trick Room
    
- **Logic:** HB bulk is set so that after the Electric Seed boost, Rillaboom’s _U-turn_ (A165) allows Shadow Shield to recover to full. I moved away from heavy SpD investment toward SpA because I felt Lunala doesn't win games where it simply tanks _Astral Barrage_. Increased power made it better at pushing through Zamazenta, Rillaboom, and Ogerpon. Speed IV 29 was used for mirrors; by being slower, it performs better when the opponent tries to reverse the field with their own Trick Room.
    

#### **Whimsicott**

- **Nature:** Calm
    
- **Stats:** 167-*-105-105-132-136
    
- **EVs:** 252 HP / 0 Def / 60 SpA / 196 SpD / 0 Spe
    
- **Tera Type:** Water
    
- **Item:** Covert Cloak
    
- **Moves:** Moonblast, Encore, Light Screen, Tailwind
    
- **Logic:** SpA was invested enough to change the roll for _Moonblast_ chip damage against Shadow Rider cores. I wanted to outspeed Booster Energy Flutter Mane in Tailwind, but the EVs were too tight, so I prioritized bulk.
    

#### **Urshifu (Rapid Strike)**

- **Nature:** Adamant
    
- **Stats:** 185-198-121-*-81-141
    
- **EVs:** 76 HP / 236 Atk / 4 Def / 0 SpA / 4 SpD / 188 Spe
    
- **Tera Type:** Ghost
    
- **Item:** Choice Band
    
- **Moves:** Surging Strikes, Close Combat, Aqua Jet, U-turn
    
- **Logic:** Speed outruns Max Speed Smeargle by 2 points. Under Tailwind, it outruns Modest Choice Scarf Miraidon.
    

#### **Incineroar**

- **Nature:** Adamant
    
- **Stats:** 202-150-118-*-118-96
    
- **EVs:** 252 HP / 12 Atk / 60 Def / 0 SpA / 60 SpD / 124 Spe
    
- **Tera Type:** Bug
    
- **Item:** Assault Vest (Note: Article says "Assault Vest" but moveset includes Fake Out/Parting Shot; context implies "Choice Scarf" or high Speed was the focus, but text specifically says Assault Vest in Japanese context. _Correction based on text: HD is set to survive Specs Miraidon's Electro Drift._)
    
- **Moves:** Flare Blitz, Knock Off, U-turn, Fake Out
    
- **Logic:** Heavy Speed investment to win the lead against Miraidon + Incineroar mirrors. If Incineroar is faster, it can _Knock Off_ a Choice Specs from the opponent while Miraidon protects. Most Japanese Incineroars were S94-95, so S96 was a perfect benchmark.
    

#### **Ursaluna (Normal Form)**

- **Nature:** Adamant
    
- **Stats:** 233-209-130-*-102-71
    
- **EVs:** 220 HP / 236 Atk / 36 Def / 0 SpA / 12 SpD / 4 Spe
    
- **Tera Type:** Ghost
    
- **Item:** Flame Orb
    
- **Moves:** Facade, Headlong Rush, Earthquake, Protect
    
- **Logic:** HB survives Adamant Urshifu-R _Surging Strikes_. HD survives Modest Miraidon _Draco Meteor_. I did not use Minimum Speed because I wanted to outspeed Rillaboom under Tailwind against Shadow Rider/Clefairy teams.

### **■ Selections and Strategies**

**VS Life Orb Shadow Rider + Zamazenta (+ Rillaboom/Brute Bonnet/Raging Bolt/Ogerpon)**

- **Lead:** Miraidon + Lunala | **Back:** Incineroar + Ursaluna
    
- Chip with the leads and aim for an Ursaluna sweep. Don't Tera early; the key is to spam _Wide Guard_ against Shadow Rider.
    
- **Alt:** Lunala + Incineroar | **Back:** Miraidon + Ursaluna
    
    Strong against leads other than Shadow Rider + Zamazenta. Against Rillaboom + Zamazenta, switching Incineroar to Miraidon is strong, though skilled players may use _U-turn_ to read it.
    

**VS Focus Sash Shadow Rider + Zamazenta (+ Chien-Pao/Amoonguss)**

- **Lead:** Incineroar + Miraidon | **Back:** Lunala + Ursaluna
    
- The leads handle almost any variation. In the mid-game, you can chip away using Lunala's _Wide Guard_ and Ursaluna's _Earthquake_ even without Trick Room.
    

**VS Ice Rider + Miraidon (+ Incineroar/Urshifu/Landorus/Amoonguss)**

- **Lead:** Miraidon + Incineroar | **Back:** Lunala + Urshifu
    
- If Miraidon is Scarf, Electric Tera Miraidon survives _Draco Meteor_. If you KO their Miraidon, Urshifu and Incineroar become very dominant.
    

**VS Koraidon + Flutter Mane (+ Ice Rider/Raging Bolt)**

- **Lead:** Whimsicott + Miraidon | **Back:** Lunala + Incineroar
    
- If you face Koraidon + Flutter Mane, Flutter Mane usually wants to _Moonblast_ Miraidon. Using _Light Screen_ + _Draco Meteor_ is strong (if Miraidon outspeeds Koraidon).
    
- **Alt:** Miraidon + Whimsicott | **Back:** Lunala + Ursaluna
    
    Strong against Raging Bolt or Koraidon _Protect_. Unfavorable if both Ice Rider and Incineroar are brought.
    

**VS Shadow Rider + Incineroar/Rillaboom/Clefairy + Miraidon**

- **Lead:** Miraidon + Incineroar | **Back:** Whimsicott + Ursaluna
    
- If they lead Shadow Rider to setup, bring Whimsicott + Ursaluna. If they use _Fake Out_ + _Nasty Plot_, you can counter with _Encore_ + _Facade_.

Practice before Worlds felt great, but I was nervous after failing to win NAIC, Nationals, or the Terurun Festival.
