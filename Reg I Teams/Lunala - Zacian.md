### **[Team Building History]**

More than half a year into the "Double Restricted" (Reg I) environment, "standard" builds have come to dominate the meta. I tried using top-tier teams like Shadow Rider/Zamazenta and Miraidon/Ice Rider, but they didn't feel right. I decided to build a team specifically designed to counter the meta.

1. **Countering Shadow Rider + Zamazenta:** Since this is the most popular core, I needed answers for it. I realized my playstyle requires a **Normal-type** to immune Ghost moves. I considered:
    
    - **Terapagos:** Too passive for me; I dislike "stalling/stacking" builds.
        
    - **Bloodmoon Ursaluna:** Strong independently, but slow.
        
    - **Indeedee/Farigiraf:** Good vs. Priority, but lacked individual power.
        
    - **Smeargle:** High support power via _Spore_. I chose Smeargle (Focus Sash) as the anchor.
        
2. **Countering Miraidon:** Referencing Grand’s WCS 2024 notes, a stable lead against Miraidon is **"Spread Move + Follow Me (with a Focus Sash/Action Guarantee)."** I looked for a Restricted with high bulk and _Dazzling Gleam_.
    
    - Miraidon was rejected because I wanted Smeargle’s _Spore_ pressure (which is hindered by Electric Terrain).
        
    - **Lunala** was the winner by process of elimination.
        
3. **Speed Control & Ghost Coverage:** I needed a Tailwind user. **Roaring Moon** was chosen because it resists Ghost (Shadow Rider) and Water (Kyogre). Unlike Tornadus, it provides immediate offensive pressure, accurate moves, and physical Dark-type damage.
    
4. **Breaking Zamazenta:** To break through Zamazenta (especially under Wide Guard), I added **Landorus-Incarnate**. It provides massive Special Attack power that synergizes perfectly with Tailwind.
    
5. **Steel Type & Priority:** To handle **Ice Rider Calyrex** (Glacial Lance spam) and priority-heavy teams (Chien-Pao + Dragonite), I added **Zacian-Crowned**. It forces Terastallization from Miraidon/Koraidon and checks most powerful standard Pokémon.
    
6. **The Final Piece:** **Hearthflame Ogerpon** was added to:
    
    - Check Koraidon/Flutter Mane Sun teams.
        
    - Break through Calm Mind builds with Tera Fire _Ivy Cudgel_.
        
    - Provide a second redirection user (_Follow Me_) against Miraidon.

### **[Common Leads and Strategies]**

#### **1. Lunala + Smeargle / Back: Any 2**

The primary lead.

- **Vs Slower Teams:** _Meteor Beam_ + _Spore_.
    
- **Vs One Fast/One Slow:** _Meteor Beam_ (into fast slot) + _Spore_ (into slow slot).
    
- **Vs Two Fast Teams:** _Meteor Beam_ + _Fake Out_ (or _Follow Me_).
    

#### **2. Roaring Moon + [Lunala/Zacian/Landorus] / Back: Any 2**

Counters teams that try to beat Smeargle with _Taunt_ or _Tornadus_. Use _Tailwind_ or _Taunt_ from Roaring Moon while the partner attacks.

---

### **[Specific Matchup Guide]**

#### **VS Shadow Rider + Zamazenta**

- **Win Rate:** Over 80% (except vs. Whimsicott variants).
    
- **Key Plays:**
    
    - **Ghost Tera Roaring Moon + Wide Guard:** This combo almost completely walls the core.
        
    - **Pivoting:** Switch Smeargle into Roaring Moon to soak up a _Heavy Slam_ aimed at Smeargle.
        
    - **Zacian Cleanup:** Zacian resists _Psychic_ and handles the late game.
        

#### **VS Miraidon + Lunala (Federico Style)**

- **Lead:** Lunala + Smeargle | **Back:** Ogerpon + Landorus
    
- **Strategy:** _Meteor Beam_ + _Fake Out_ into Miraidon. If Smeargle goes down, bring in Ogerpon to use _Follow Me_ while Lunala spams _Dazzling Gleam_.
    

#### **VS Ice Rider Calyrex**

- **Lead:** Smeargle + Landorus | **Back:** Ogerpon + Any
    
- **Strategy:** Use Water Tera Landorus to ignore _Glacial Lance_. Landorus is vital because Fire Tera Ice Rider is extremely hard for this team to hit otherwise.
    

#### **VS Terapagos**

- **Strategy:** Slightly difficult. Use Roaring Moon to _Taunt_ the _Calm Mind_ setup. Close the game with Zacian’s _Sacred Sword_ or Ogerpon’s _Ivy Cudgel_.
    

---

### **[Major Weaknesses]**

- **Flutter Mane:** Its Speed is a nightmare for this team. Roaring Moon hates being on the field with it.
    
- **Fire-types:** Only _Meteor Beam_ and Landorus hit them hard. You must manage your Terastallization carefully.
    
- **Ice Rider Spam:** If you lose the position to stop _Glacial Lance_, the game ends quickly.
    

---

### **[Final Words]**

This team is exceptionally strong against Shadow Rider + Zamazenta. If you are looking for a meta-counter that rewards strong positioning and prediction, give this a try!

After scrutiny, I chose **Bloodmoon Ursaluna and Smeargle** as the starting point. (Eventually, it became just Smeargle.)

Next, I considered Miraidon systems, the second most common after Shadow/Zama. Referencing Grand's Reg G article on Miraidon counters: **"Spread Move (Glacial Lance) + Follow Me (with action guarantee)"** is a stable lead. This principle is cited in Tell’s qualifier videos and Kogepan’s WCS notes; many understand it intuitively. Since Smeargle was a candidate for the "action guarantee" Follow Me slot, I officially adopted **Smeargle @ Focus Sash**.

Next, I considered which spread move to spam with Follow Me:

1. Astral Barrage, 2. Precipice Blades, 3. Sandsear Storm, 4. Glacial Lance, 5. Dazzling Gleam.

I rejected 1 because Kinugawa's Shadow Rider/Smeargle build is already optimized; I felt my version would just be a worse copy. 4 (Ice Rider) is solid on paper, but Miraidon players obviously prepare for it (Incineroar + Urshifu-R are 90% present). Thus, I chose **Fairy-type spread (Dazzling Gleam) + Follow Me**. I wanted Flutter Mane, but in Reg I, using a "low bulk, high speed" Mane as a main Miraidon counter against high-bulk TR Miraidon builds is tough. I searched for a Restricted with high bulk + Dazzling Gleam:

1. Miraidon, 2. Lunala.
