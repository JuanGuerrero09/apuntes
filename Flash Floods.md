A flash flood is a flood with rapid onset, following:
- a heavy rainfall
- failure of a dam, levee or other structure that is impounding water
- suden rise of water level associated with river ice jams
Associated with small and fast-response basins. Life-threatening flood that begins within 6 hours (even 3 hours)

Challenges:
- Significant damages at local level, irrelevant at national level
- Downscaling problem: numerical prediction at temporal and spatial scale is inappropiate for flash floods
- Needs local forecast, local knowledge and local acts
- Short lead time, accurate forecast are unlikely to happen

Soil influence:
- Soil moisture affects infiltration rate
- Soil texture (eg. clay) causes low infiltration rates, more rapid runoff

Basin influences:
- Flash flooding can result from short duration rainfall and may occur in areas with dry soil or even drought conditions
- Is for small basins with relatively steep slopes, low roughness and increased imperviousness

Managing flash floods:
- Structural measures
	- Delay speed of surface runoff: terraced farm crops, stone walls, forested lands and pastures
	- Regulating river and streams
	- Shaping retentions /adding retention reservoirs
	- Improve river conservation
- Non-structural measures
	- Flash flood forecasting and warning system
	- Awareness raising
	- Land use planning

Early warning
- Numerical weather prediction
- Rainfall forecast
- Hydrological model
- Hydrological forecast


**Flash flood guidance (FFG)**

FFG is a numerical estimate of the average rainfall over a specified area and  time duration required to initiate flooding on small streams 

e.g. If the 3‐hour FFG is 25 mm then flooding should begin on small streams  when rainfall in a 3‐hour period > 25 mm 

Application: 
- Applies to a basin for a given rainfall duration  
- An inverse approach: find the rainfall that causes flooding  
- FFD is computed in two stages
	- Compute the threshold discharge
	- Compute the corresponding rainfall
- Threshold discharge (TQ)
	- Example: stream reaches flood  stage in 3 hr with basin‐ averaged runoff of 15 m3/s   3 hr threshold discharge is 15  m3/s
