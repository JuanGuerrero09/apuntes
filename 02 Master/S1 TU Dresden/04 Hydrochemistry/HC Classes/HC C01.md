### Bonds and electrons

### Molecular Structure of Water

- Oxygen has **6 valence electrons**, needs **2 more** to complete the octet.
- The **O atom** forms two **covalent bonds** with hydrogen and keeps two **lone pairs**.
- The resulting **bond angle** is **104.5¬∞**, forming a **bent molecule**.
- This geometry creates a **dipole** ‚Äî a separation of partial charges (Œ¥‚Å∫ on H, Œ¥‚Åª on O).

The **104.5¬∞** angle is the key to life. Because the molecule is "bent" rather than linear (like $CO_2$, which is $180^\circ$ and non-polar), the dipoles do not cancel out.

- **The Dipole Moment:** The oxygen atom "hogs" the electrons, becoming partially negative ($\delta^-$), while the hydrogens are partially positive ($\delta^+$).
- **Lone Pairs:** Oxygen has **two lone pairs** of electrons. These occupy space and "push" the H-O-H bonds downward, which is why the angle is 104.5¬∞ instead of a perfect tetrahedral 109.5¬∞.

### Hydrogen Bonds (H-Bonds)

- Result from electrostatic attraction between the **partial positive (H‚Å∫)** and **partial negative (O‚Åª)** regions of different molecules.
- Responsible for many **unique properties** of water:
    - High boiling and melting points.
    - Surface tension and cohesion.
    - **Negative thermal expansion**: water expands when freezing.
    - Density maximum at **4 ¬∞C** (important for aquatic life).

### Crystal Structure of Ice

- Forms a **tetrahedral lattice** with both **covalent** and **hydrogen bonds** (bond angle ‚âà 109.5¬∞).
- Creates open spaces ‚Üí **ice is less dense than water**, allowing it to float.
- **Density Anomaly:** In liquid water, molecules are close but disorganized. As water cools toward **4 ¬∞C**, they pack tighter. Below 4 ¬∞C, H-bonds begin to force the molecules into a **rigid hexagonal lattice (Ice)**. This lattice contains a lot of "empty space," making ice ~9% less dense than liquid water.

### Stratification in Lakes

Due to density differences:

- **4 ¬∞C** water stays at the bottom (most dense).
- Ice and cold water remain on top, insulating deeper layers.
- This allows aquatic life to survive through winter.
- **Dimictic lakes** mix twice a year (spring & autumn).

# üê¢ Definitions

- **Electronegativity:** Tendency of an atom to attract shared electrons.
- **Dipole:** Separation of partial charges within a molecule.
- **Dispersion forces (London):** Weak attractions from temporary dipoles.
- **Enthalpy (H):** Total heat content at constant pressure.
- **Entropy (S):** Measure of disorder/randomness.
- **Vapor pressure:** Pressure of vapor in equilibrium with its liquid/solid.
- **Surface tension:** Tendency to minimize surface area due to cohesion.
- **Gibbs energy (G):** Criterion of spontaneity at constant T,P.
- **Speciation:** Distribution of an element among chemical forms (e.g., $\mathrm{NH_3/NH_4^+}$, $\mathrm{CO_2/HCO_3^-/CO_3^{2-}}$).
- **Buffer capacity:** Resistance of a solution to pH changes (in natural waters, dominated by the inorganic carbon system).
- **Alkalinity:** Capacity to neutralize acid (mainly $\mathrm{HCO_3^-}$, $\mathrm{CO_3^{2-}}$, $\mathrm{OH^-}$).
- **Calco-carbonic equilibrium:** Equilibria of $\mathrm{Ca^{2+}}$ / $\mathrm{HCO_3^-}$ / $\mathrm{CO_3^{2-}}$ with $\mathrm{CaCO_3(s)}$ (predicts scaling/dissolution).
- **Redox potential (Eh):** Tendency to gain/lose electrons (oxidizing vs reducing conditions).
