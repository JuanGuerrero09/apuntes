#### Br√∏nsted-Lowry acid/base theory

$$
\underbrace{Acid}_{\text{proton donor}} \rightleftharpoons H^++\underbrace{Base}_{\text{proton acceptor}} \quad \text{conjugat acid/base pair}
$$
The ability to donate or to accept  protons determines if a substance acts as acid or base. Acids and bases are  not defined as ‚Äúfixed‚Äù substance classes. They are defined according to their  function.  
e.g.
$$ \begin{gather}
H_2SO_4 \rightleftharpoons H^+ + HSO_4^-\\
NH_4^+ \rightleftharpoons H^+ + NH_3\\
HCO_3 \rightleftharpoons H^+ + CO_3^{2-}\\
\end{gather}
$$

##### Autoprotolysis of water
Water acts both as an acid and as a base. It is an **ampholyte**. In this case,  protolysis takes place between two identical molecules. Such a proton transfer  reaction is referred to as autoprotolysis. 

- Proton release - H2O <-> H+ + OH-
- Proton acceptance - H2O + H+ <-> H3O-
- Autoprotolysis?? H2O + H2O <-> OH- + H3O+ ??

Free hydrogen ions do not exist in aqueous systems. As H+ ions are formed, they are  initially hydrated by one water molecule to form the hydronium ion (H3O+). Then the hydronium ion is further hydrated: 

primary hydration sphere: 3 water molecules are bound by  hydrogen bridges H9O4 +   

outer hydration sphere: further water molecules can be  bound to the hydronium ion; the exact hydration number is  unknown
