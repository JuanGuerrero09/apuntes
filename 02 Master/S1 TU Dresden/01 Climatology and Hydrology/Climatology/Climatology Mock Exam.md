1. Name the law that characterizes the power radiated from a black body in terms of its temperature.
- **Stefan-Boltzmann Law**.

2. Name the law that states the wavelength of highest intensity of a black body emitter of radiation?
    - **Wien's Displacement Law**.

3. How many times larger is the solar constant $S_0$ compared to the average radiation gain at the outer atmosphere of the Earth?
    - **4 times larger**. (Calculated by the ratio of the Earth's cross-section area to its total surface area).
    
4. Sort the following surface types by **ascending** albedo: Dry soil, Coniferous trees, Short grass, Deciduous trees.
    - **Coniferous trees (0.05–0.15)** $\rightarrow$ **Deciduous trees (0.15–0.20)** $\rightarrow$ **Short grass (0.25)** $\rightarrow$ **Dry soil (0.40)**.
    
5. Turbulence is the dominant transport mechanism in the atmospheric boundary layer. Since there is no complete definition for this phenomenon, it is rather called a "syndrome." Name five criteria for this turbulence syndrome.
    - **Irregularity**, **Diffusivity (Mixing)**, **Large Reynolds Numbers**, **Three-dimensional Vorticity**, and **Dissipativity**.
    
6. What is the main flow direction of a mountain breeze system? Is it along-valley or cross-valley? What are favorable meteorological conditions for the development of such a circulation?
    - The main flow direction is **along-valley**. Favorable conditions include **clear skies** (high radiation) and **weak background (synoptic) wind**.
    
7. Determine the latent heat flux $\lambda E$ according to the Penman-Monteith method for a meadow with the following parameters: $R_n - G = 209$, $e_s - e_a = 0.370$, $\Delta = 0.0864$, $\gamma = 0.0628$, $r_a = 148$, $r_c = 70$, $\rho = 1.21$, $c_p = 1013$.
    - **$Q_E \approx 118.06 \, W \cdot m^{-2}$**.
    
8. What are the meteorological conditions that control which one of the two types of mesoscale circulations—"urban plume" or "urban dome"—will develop?
    - **Urban Dome:** Develops under **calm/weak wind** and high radiation (vertical circulation).
    - **Urban Plume:** Develops when there is **significant background wind**, transporting the city's air horizontally.

9. Name the different layers of the typical daytime atmosphere over a city and identify the main structural differences compared to a forest.
    - **Layers:** Free Atmosphere, Entrainment Zone, Mixed Layer, Urban Boundary Layer (UBL), Inertial Sublayer (ISL), Roughness Sublayer (RSL), and Urban Canopy Layer (UCL).
    - **Differences:** Cities have a **UCL** composed of buildings, a **Roughness Sublayer** that is significantly deeper (2–5x building height), and **Anthropogenic Heat ($Q_F$)** within the canopy, which does not exist in forests.

10. Interpret the energy and carbon flux figures for Graswang and Fendt. Identify the land-cover type and provide reasons for the guess.
    - **Energy Interpretation:** The smooth curves indicate **clear-sky summer days**. Both sites show a **low Bowen Ratio** ($Q_H < Q_E$), meaning most energy is used for transpiration.
    - **Carbon Interpretation:** **Graswang** is a more productive site than Fendt, exhibiting a higher negative $CO_2$ net intake (sink).
    - **Land-Cover Guess:** **Grassland / Meadow**.
    - **Reasons:** High $CO_2$ uptake (vegetation), dominance of latent heat (transpiration), and a significant **Ground Heat Flux ($Q_G$)**, which indicates a short canopy that allows radiation to reach the soil surface.

Net radiation (Rn)
→ controlled by albedo, clouds, season

High Rn → dark surface, low albedo

Low Rn → bright surface (snow, ice, desert sand)

Sensible heat (H)
→ heating the air

High H → dry, warm surface, little evaporation

Low H → moist surface or water

Latent heat (LE)
→ evaporation / transpiration

High LE → water availability (soil moisture, vegetation, open water)

Near zero LE → dry or frozen surface

Ground heat flux (G)
→ heat stored in the surface

High G → bare soil, sand, sparse vegetation

Low G → vegetation, water, snow (insulation)
