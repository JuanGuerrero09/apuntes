a) Flow at a 4-way intersection on an  orthogonal street grid with above-roof flow diagonal to the grid → combination of helical flows in both streets generate upwards tilted conveyor belt → lifts pollutants to above-roof level b) Same, but at a T-junction → increased wind speed in receiving canyon → stronger conveyor belt 

**Roughness**

Wind profile: • Dampened inside the urban canopy layer (UCL) • Inflection point at the top of the UCL • Adapts to log-law in the roughness sublayer (RSL) 

a) Model patern b) Almost no momentum flux at ½ zH (zH:  average building height) Reduced above zH = 1 (some tall buildings) c) Momentum is transported by turbulent eddies above in RSL and ISL Transport efficiency reduced in UCL d) Sweeps bring eddies with higher velocity downwards (positive values, dominant in  UCL and lower RSL) Ejections bring lower momentum upwards (dominant in upper RSL, ISL)

### Urban heat island

Typical urban heat island (UHI): • Daytime: • Surface temperature higher in builtup area than rural area • Air temperature similar in built-up and rural areas • pronounced UHI in surface temperature (UHISurf) but not in the canopy layer (UHIUCL) • Nighttime: • Surface and air temperature in builtup area higher than in rural area • Pronounced UHISurf and UHI
