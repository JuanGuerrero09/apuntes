### Consolidated Notes (34 Questions)

1. **Describe the three terms of the vulnerability concept:**
    
    - **Value:** The importance, worth, or usefulness of something (economic, social, or ecological).
    - **Susceptibility:** The state or fact of being likely or liable to be influenced or harmed by something.
    - **Coping capacity:** The ability to deal effectively with something difficult and manage the aftermath.
    
2. **Two steps for the asset analysis, and the data sources that can be used:**
    
    - 1. **Land cover classification** (for a spatial differentiation of vulnerability).
    - 2. Data mining on socio-economic statistical sources to derive **economic land use values**.

3. **Explain the three dimensions of vulnerability:**
    
    - The intersection of **Coping capacity x Function x Susceptibility** across **Social, economic, and physical** dimensions.
        
4. **What are engineering resilience of building types?**
    
    - **Flood protection:** External defense structures (e.g., dikes).
        
    - **Flood resistance:** Preventing water from entering (dry-proofing).
        
    - **Flood resilience:** Minimizing impact and facilitating recovery (wet-proofing).
        
5. **Which are the three categories of flood damage in buildings?**
    
    - Moisture / water damages.
        
    - Statical damages (Foundation, hydrostatic and flotsam damage, overloaded building components).
        
    - Contamination damages (e.g., oil, chemicals).
        
6. **Why is building taxonomy a prerequisite for damage simulation of buildings?**
    
    - It is used to create a building type matrix to identify representative buildings for large-scale modeling.
        
7. **Mention four factors of building taxonomy:**
    
    - **Geometric:** (Area, elongatedness, height).
        
    - **Surface:** (Roof slope, roof vertices form).
        
    - **Topology:** (Compactness, adjacency).
        
8. **Which variables are important for static and dynamic floods?**
    
    - Rainfall, topography, soil, land use, flood movement, and impact.
        
9. **How do variables behave in the two flood types (Flash vs. Plain)?**
    
    - **Flash floods (dynamic):** High velocity, high energy, short warning time.
        
    - **Plain floods (static):** Slow rise, long duration, high water depth.
        
10. **Difference between wet proofing and dry proofing?**
    
    - **Dry proofing:** Keeping water out via permanent or mobile barrier systems.
        
    - **Wet proofing:** Allowing water in but using water-proof materials and relocating/elevating central heating and utilities.
        
11. **Steps to derive a depth-damage function?**
    
    - 1. Building taxonomy & determination of representative buildings.
    - 2. Simulation of synthetic flood stages and damage types (moisture, statistical, contamination).
    - 3. Specification and monetization of replacement/repair costs.
    - 4. Correlation of monetary damage to flood stage/water depth per building type.

12. **Describe two functions of MCE (Multi-criteria Evaluation):**
    
    - **MADM:** Finite alternatives with weights for rankings.
        
    - **MODM:** Infinite/continuous alternatives using linear programming (optimization).
        
13. **What are the general MCE Steps?**
    
    - 1. Impact analysis (assignment of criteria + individual evaluation).
    - 2. Equity analysis (assignment of weights + ranking).
    
14. **Definition of risk evaluation:**
    
    - The systematic investigation of an evaluand's worth and merits, where **risk** is the probability of negative consequences.
        
15. **Variables defined in the Swiss matrix for risk hazard with units:**
    
    - **Probability:** Years. High (1-50 yr), Medium (100-200 yr), Low (300+ yr).
        
    - **Intensity:** Depth ($d$) and Flow Velocity ($v \cdot d$).
        
16. **Intensity thresholds in the Swiss Matrix:**
    
    - **Strong:** $d > 2m$ or $v \cdot d > 2 m^2/s$.
        
    - **Medium:** $d > 0.5m$ or $v \cdot d > 0.5 m^2/s$.
        
    - **Low:** $d < 0.5m$ and $v \cdot d < 0.5 m^2/s$.
        
17. **What is efficiency in FRM?**
    
    - Measured as **cost-effectiveness** (expenditure vs. outcomes) or **benefit-cost ratio** (monetary benefits vs. costs).
        
18. **Analyse the first step of multi-criteria evaluation (Impact Analysis):**
    
    - Determination of relevant indicators/criteria, definition of targets/thresholds, and calculation of individual indicators.
        
19. **Analyse the second step of multi-criteria evaluation (Equity Analysis):**
    
    - Adjustment of weights for each indicator/criteria to reach a final evaluation or ranking.
        
20. **What is risk analysis and how is it done?**
    
    - The structured process of identifying and assessing potential threats. It is done by calculating the probability of an event and its potential consequences ($R = P \times C$).
        
21. **Five steps of scenario development (Parameterized regional futures):**
    
    - 1. Delineation and description of the system.
    - 2. Coupled modeling for system simulation.
    - 3. Formulation/parameterization of scenarios.
    - 4. Composition of futures.
    - 5. Ex-ante analysis and evaluation.
22. **In which framework are the five scenario steps embedded?**
    
    - They are embedded in the **Scenario Planning** framework (Development and Analysis phases).
        
23. **Definition of scenario:**
    
    - A coherent, consistent, and plausible description of a possible future situation based on specific assumptions.
        
24. **How is scenario development done, what are storylines?**
    
    - Storylines describe developments and relationships between system elements (factors) prone to change, which are then shown in projections.
        
25. **How is scenario planning done?**
    
    - Done in two phases: **Scenario Development** (system delineation, drivers, projections) and **Scenario Analysis**.
        
26. **Types of scenarios:**
    
    - **Explorative:** Forecasting/projections of current trends.
        
    - **Normative:** Target-reaching or futures to avoid.
        
    - **Policy scenarios:** Testing intended policy actions.
        
27. **Differences between land cover and land use:**
    
    - **Land cover:** Observable physical/biological cover (soil, vegetation).
        
    - **Land use:** Human arrangements, activities, and objectives on that surface.
        
28. **How is LU/LC projected?**
    
    - 1. Retrospective analysis. 2. Allocation of LC class changes. 3. Projection of demand for LC classes.
            
29. **Differences between measures and instruments?**
    
    - **Measures:** Direct biophysical impact (dikes).
        
    - **Instruments:** Indirect mechanisms triggering impact (regulations, incentives).
        
30. **What are the categories of measures?**
    
    - Adaptation (biophysical), Control (built structures), and Retreat (relocation).
        
31. **What are the categories of instruments?**
    
    - Regulation (laws), Stimulation (financial), Compensation (risk distribution), and Information (hazard maps).
        
32. **Explain the SPRC model:**
    
    - **Source:** Origin (Rain).
        
    - **Pathway:** Route (Inundation).
        
    - **Receptor:** Entity affected (People/Property).
        
    - **Consequence:** Final impact (Economic/Social loss).
        
33. **What are the intervention forms?**
    
    - **Pre-event:** Prevention, Protection, Preparedness.
        
    - **Event:** Defense, Warning, Response.
        
    - **Post-event:** Recovery.
        
34. **Types of actors relevant in IWRM:**
    
    - State/regional authorities, municipalities, citizens, businesses, and scientific organizations.
        
35. **What are networks and which types are there?**
    
    - Social/organizational structures: **Evolutionary** (emergent) or **Goal-directed** (planned).
        
36. **What are strategies?**
    
    - Social processes where diverse stakeholders come together to design plan-making processes.
        
37. **Describe three instruments of risk evaluation (EU Directive):**
    
    - Preliminary flood risk map, Flood hazard/risk maps, and FRM Plans.
        
38. **Importance of public participation in FRM:**
    
    - Ensures communication of facts, improves plan plausibility, and increases overall benefits/acceptance.
        
39. **What is primary vs. secondary legislation?**
    
    - **Primary:** Treaties (Constitutional law, e.g., Lisbon).
        
    - **Secondary:** Regulations (direct), Directives (transposed), and Decisions.
        
40. **What are the institutions of the EU?**
    
    - Parliament (Citizens), Council (Member States), Commission (Union interest).
        
41. **What is the principle of solidarity?**
    
    - Measures in one state should not significantly increase flood risk for neighbors (upstream/downstream).
        
42. **Why is spatial planning important for FRM?**
    
    - It coordinates differing demands, resolves spatial conflicts, and safeguards land use functions.
