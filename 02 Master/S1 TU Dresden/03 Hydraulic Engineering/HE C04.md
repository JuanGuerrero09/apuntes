### Scouring at Piers/Bridges
Types of scour:
- Local scour?  Erosion occurring directly around bridge piers or
abutments due to flow acceleration and vortex formation. The localized
scour is the most common scour happening to bridges.
	- Clearwaters scour  No sediment is deposited in the scouring area
from the upstream side of the river
	- Live-Bed Scour Sediments are continuously supplied from the
upstream to the scour zone.
- Contraction scour Erosion resulting from the narrowing of a river or
stream, which increases flow velocity and sediment transport capacity
- General scour Overall degradation of the riverbed across a bridge
site, often due to natural changes in the river's flow regime.
