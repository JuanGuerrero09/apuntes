# Case Study: Alexandria, Egypt

# Challenges in Urban Flooding
- Caused by intense localized rainfall (pluvial flooding)
- Happens due urbanization and climate change
- Relative small (impervious) areas affected
- High damages and in areas of high population
- LULC Changes. increases built-up areas and reduces green space
- Urban heat islands influence rainfall over urban areas

## Data Challenges

-  Flood forecasting possible but requieres better data collection (hourly from dense gauge networks)
- Weather radar often not available
- Remotely sensed data is coarse and inaccurate

**Anticipatory Flood Management** (AFM)

In AFM you use forecast models to forecast future states (few hours to a couple of  days) and anticipate the action needed to lessen the severity of the hazard

## Forecasting - Rainfall threshold based approach

When resources are limited or data is unavailable then rainfall threshold may be  determined based on historical floods 
Thresholds may be for  Flooding (or no flooding) (compare with FFG)  Example: if rainfall in 6 hrs is more than 20 mm then there will be flooding   More complex such as No flooding, Minor flooding, Significant flooding, Severe flooding  Accuracy of thresholds depend upon how many flood data have been used in determining the  threshold(s)  It may happen that No flooding is predicted whereas there are floods (misses) and flooding is  predicted and there are no floods (false alarms)  When combined with NWP output can be used for forecasting

**Challenges in modelling flood inundation**

 Technical challenges: major improvements in recent times  Data issues: Requires high resolution DEM (digital elevation  model)  Free DEMs: ~30mx30m or ~90mx90m  Without better DEM model results may not be believable   Possible to buy (JAXA and other sources) 


**Validation**

 Pluvial floods often have short duration  Typical convective rainstorms in the  Mediterranean region (or elsewhere) may last  for 1 hour/ 2 hours   If flooded then inundation may not last for  days/ weeks  Satellite passes may miss the flooding  How do you validate your flood inundation  model? This is more challenging for pluvial  flooding than for other types of floods 


## Summary of challenges

 Urbanization, population growth/ migration, climate change leading to increased  urban flood risk   Data challenges: hydro‐meteo and catchment data resolution often too coarse;  limitation in data sharing  Forecasting: required spatial and temporal resolution is challenging  Decision making with uncertain forecast is challenging  Anticipatory flood management plays more and more important role but requires  reliable models
