# Flood Directive

Introduced in 2007, following a catastrophic flood in 2002 in Europe

Purpose: Establish framework for assessment and management of flood risk


## Preliminary flood risk assessment

Carry out preliminary flood risk assessment for each river basin by 2011

## Flood risk mapping

All EU countries to develop flood hazard and flood risk maps for each river basin by 2013.
- Develop flood maps for: Floods of low, medium and high probability
- Provide following information: Flood extent, water depths and flow velocity (where possible)

It shows adverse consequences in:
- Number of inhabitants affected
- Economic activity affected
- Installations which might cause accidental pollution
- Any other useful information

Should always show:
- Flood extent
- Water depth or water level
- Number of inhabitants affected
- Type of economic area affected
- Potential pollution sources listed in WFD Annex IV

May show where appropiate:
- Water velocity or flow
- Other information considered useful: debris, sediments, other pollution sources

# Flood Maps

- Flood plain map: Indicates area that may be covered by a flood (with flood extent)
- Flood hazard map: Flood plain map complemented with (some):
	- Type of flood, probability
	- Inundation depth and velocity
	- Road networks, evacuation routes, etc
- Flood risk map: Flood hazard map with
	- Number of inhabitants potentially affected
	- flood defense assets
	- economic and hazardous activities

# Developing flood risk maps

Steps:
- Prepare accurate DTM
- Carry out:
	- Hydrological modelling
	- Frequency analysis
	- inundation modelling
- Collect other socio-economic data
- Prepare maps
- Disseminate

**DEM**

- Free SRTM provide 90x90m, ASTER 30x30m
- The requirement of accuracy is much higher: H 5x5 and V 50 cm
- Data might be collected as one or combination of: LIDAR and SAR

**Hydrological model**

- Regionalisation approach: Using gauge data
- Rainfall-runoff modelling: Different types of models, and number of tools available (HEC-HMS, NAM, etc)
- Frequency may be 10, 50, 100 and extreme

**Socio-economic data**

- Population data (required to be provided at high resolution, if unavailable use land use as proxy)
- Economic data

**Hydraulic modelling**
- Provide: level of inundation, flood depth and velocity
- Variety of tools available: HEC-RAS, MIKE11
- Data requirement: flood discharge (corresponding to a probability), channel and lood plain geometry
