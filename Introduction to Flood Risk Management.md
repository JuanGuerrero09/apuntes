
Floods are rare event, but the consequences are huge.
There's a tendency of increasing risk from flooding, that can happen due increased flooding (**hazard**) or due increased **vulnerability**.

Why urbanization is changing rainfall?

## Three phases of FRM

### Pre-flood measures

Preventive risk management: spatial planning, flood defence measures, etc

### Operational flood management:
- MOST IMPORTANT: Detection of likelihood of a flood forming
- See if is necessary to operate flood control structures
- Warning issued

### Post flood measures
Disaster relief, restructuring, recover and review of the flood management activities.


## Integrated flood management

Managing floods:
- Structural measures - dams, diversions, embankments
- Non-structural measures - regulation, forecasting and warning, flood insurance
- Do nothing

Integrated flood management includes:
- Land use management
- Water resources management
- Risk management

Climate change, population growth/ migration and economic activities increase  the risk from flooding. Absolute protection from flooding is technically infeasible and economically and environmentally unviable 


## Defining Risk

There's conflict between the usage of flood plain for **human activities** and as a **passage of flood water**. Risk is a human perception, no human, no risk.

Flood risk changes due:
- Change in climate, land use
- Population or economic growth
- Risk mitigation measures

Usually the **Hazard- Exposure- Vulnerability** model is employed to represent risk:
- Hazard: Threatening natural event
- Exposure: assets/human present at location of the hazard
- Vulnerability: Degree of damage multiplied by value

## FRM Fundamentals

Knowledge about hazards should include:
- Type of floods (fluvial / coastal / flash / pluvial)
- Probability of a particular flood event
- Flood magnitude, extent
- Water depth
- Flow velocity

FRN aims to lower damage and loss by lowering flooding probability and reducing its consequence


## Risk Assessment

### Generation of floods and types

River floods: Generated by prolonged and intense rainfall, rainfall by monsoons, hurricanes and typhoons.

The generation of flood is due the rainfall-runoff process, the propagation of flood is as disturbance of flow in open channels

Flooding types:
- Coastal floods: storm surges
- Riverine/fluvial floods: large areas in lower catchments, slow rise
- Flash floods: localised and fast
- Pluvial floods, much influence in urban areas

Hazard:
- Identify flooding types
- Identify corresponding frequencies
- Identify flooding extent, depth, velocities and duration
Vulnerability:
- Reliability of assets
- Identify receptors

Then:
- Assess damages
- Estimate future changes
- Plan for residual risk

Assessment typically uses **Source-Pathway-Receptor-Consequence** model

### Flooding Types

#### Coastal floods

- Storm surges due cyclones, hurricanes and typhoons
- Swollen water may overtop or breach protection and inundate inland areas
- Affect large areas
- Wind speed main problem, very high
- Evacuation difficult due weather conditions but needed

#### Fluvial floods

- Intense rainfall in river basin
- Large areas in large catchments affected
- Slow rise, now forecasting days ahead possible
- Fatality low, damage high
- Evacuation possible but long flood duration could be problematic

#### Flash floods

- Small catchments, hilly terrain and ungauged
- Localiz ed
- Difficult to forecast but high dependence on forecasting
- Depends upon accuracy of rainfall forecast
- Structural measures are difficult to implement
- Silent killer

#### Pluvial floods

- Caused by intense localized rainfall
- Affects urban areas, since they are more impermeable, relatively small areas are affected
- Flooding possibility depends on stormwater drainage capacity
- High damages not many fatalities
- Forecasting possible but requires better data collection


### Frequency analysis

The flood frequency requires past flood data. Provides flood magnitudes (discharges) with return period or probability. 

### Inundation Modelling

Solve shallow water equations (saint venants) in momentum and continuity. Provides information about flood extent and depth for a probability. Also velocity information can be provided.

### Reliability of assets

Flood defense assets should be regularly maintained and monitored. Reliability should be checked with increased load due to high water level.

### Risk mapping

Risk maps help in identifying critical areas requiring restrictions for further development, forecasting needs and evaluation planning

### Damage assessment

The assessment of flood damage in the connection with flood risk management we combine:
- Hydrological knowledge
- Inundation modelling
- Damages corresponding to flood depth

## Risk Mitigation

Risk can be reduced by:
- reducing flood **probability** (hazard)
- reducing **consequences** (exposure and vulnerability): also easier than reducing 
No fixed best solution, is location-specific and depends on stakeholders and economy

Risk mitigation measurements:
- Structural: direct intervention
- Non-structural and policy instruments: spatial planning


**Source-Pathway-Receptor-Consequence model**
